    Answers to the most asked questions

    When I set my prices, I focus on balancing my experience, the value I bring, and what’s competitive in the market. I start by researching what others in my field charge for similar services. I also factor in the time, effort, and unique skills I bring to the table. Initially, I aim for rates that attract clients, but I adjust them as I gain more experience, build my portfolio, and receive feedback. My goal is to ensure my pricing reflects the quality and professionalism I deliver.

    When I start a job, I begin by thoroughly understanding the client’s requirements and goals. Communication is key, so I make sure to clarify all details and expectations before moving forward. Next, I create a plan or draft for the work and share it with the client for approval. Throughout the project, I provide regular updates and remain open to feedback to ensure we’re on the same page. Once the work is complete, I review everything carefully and deliver the final result, making sure it meets or exceeds expectations.

    The time each service takes depends on its complexity and scope. For smaller tasks, like simple graphic designs or minor content edits, I typically need 1–3 days. Larger projects, like website development or detailed marketing strategies, can take anywhere from 1–4 weeks. I always discuss timelines upfront with my clients and ensure I provide realistic deadlines while keeping room for feedback and revisions.
